From Hope to Hostility: A 60-Year Legal Shift

A detailed legal chronology of 29 key cases and regulations that shaped, eroded, and ultimately overturned the constitutional right to abortion.

Detailed Chronological Timeline

1965

Griswold v. Connecticut

**Holding:** Recognized marital privacy for contraception. **Effect:** Laid constitutional privacy groundwork for *Roe*.

1972

Eisenstadt v. Baird

**Holding:** Extended contraception rights to individuals (including unmarried). **Effect:** Expanded autonomy principle, building on *Griswold*.

1973

Roe v. Wade

**Holding:** Recognized pre-viability abortion right; established trimester framework. **Effect:** Nationwide legalization before viability.

1973

Doe v. Bolton

**Holding:** Broad health exception; struck procedural hurdles. **Effect:** Expanded practical access; reinforced *Roe*.

1976

Planned Parenthood v. Danforth

**Holding:** Struck down spousal consent; limits on parental consent. **Effect:** Protected decisional autonomy.

1976

Hyde Amendment (Regulation)

**Action:** Legislative provision barring federal funds (Medicaid) for most abortions. **Effect:** Created a major financial barrier for low-income patients (chipping away at *access*).

1977

Maher v. Roe

**Holding:** States may deny Medicaid funding for most abortions. **Effect:** Reduced access for low-income patients; distinguished funding from the right (chipping).

1980

Harris v. McRae

**Holding:** Upheld Hyde Amendment Medicaid limits. **Effect:** Confirmed a major financial barrier for poor patients; followed *Maher* (chipping).

1983

City of Akron v. Akron CRH

**Holding:** Struck down consent/waiting/hospital rules as inconsistent with Roe. **Effect:** Shielded access from burdensome regulations; reaffirmed Roe.

1986

Thornburgh v. ACOG

**Holding:** Invalidated PA informed-consent/reporting mandates. **Effect:** Blocked deterrent provisions; reaffirmed Roe.

1989

Webster v. RHS

**Holding:** Allowed 20-week viability testing; barred public funds/facilities. **Effect:** Expanded state leeway without overruling Roe; **limited Roe** (chipping).

1991

Rust v. Sullivan (Adjacent)

**Holding:** Upheld Title X counseling "gag rule." **Effect:** Restricted information in low-income clinics; adjacent chipping (did not alter the right itself).

1992

Planned Parenthood v. Casey

**Holding:** Reaffirmed core but adopted **undue-burden test**. **Effect:** Preserved the right but made regulation much easier, severely **narrowing *Roe*** (chipping).

1993

Bray v. Alexandria WHC (Adjacent)

**Holding:** No conspiracy claim for clinic blockades based on anti-abortion animus. **Effect:** Weakened federal civil-rights remedies against obstruction (adjacent chipping).

2000

Stenberg v. Carhart

**Holding:** Struck down state "partial-birth" ban lacking health exception. **Effect:** Protected providers/patients; applied *Casey*.

2003

Scheidler v. NOW (II) (Adjacent)

**Holding:** Protest conduct was not "extortion" under Hobbs Act. **Effect:** Reduced RICO leverage against clinic obstruction (adjacent chipping).

2006

Scheidler v. NOW (III) (Adjacent)

**Holding:** Reaffirmed no RICO/Hobbs route on same theory. **Effect:** Further limited anti-obstruction tools (adjacent chipping).

2006

Ayotte v. Planned Parenthood (Adjacent)

**Holding:** Instructed narrow remedies to salvage parental-notice law. **Effect:** Encouraged minimalism, allowing more restrictive laws to survive (adjacent chipping).

2007

Gonzales v. Carhart

**Holding:** Upheld federal PBA ban without health exception. **Effect:** Increased **deference to legislatures**; severely narrowed *Stenberg* (chipping).

2014

McCullen v. Coakley (Adjacent)

**Holding:** Struck 35-ft clinic buffer zones on 1A grounds. **Effect:** Harder to manage protests at clinics (adjacent chipping).

2014

Burwell v. Hobby Lobby (Adjacent)

**Holding:** RFRA allowed closely-held firms to refuse ACA contraception coverage. **Effect:** Reduced no-cost contraception for some workers; **expanded religious exemptions** (adjacent chipping).

2016

Whole Woman’s Health v. Hellerstedt

**Holding:** Struck TX admitting-privileges/ASC rules. **Effect:** Reopened path to challenge burdensome regulations; clarified *Casey* (strengthening).

2018

NIFLA v. Becerra (Adjacent)

**Holding:** Barred compelled notices by crisis-pregnancy centers. **Effect:** Weakened pro-access disclosure tools (adjacent chipping).

2019

Box v. Planned Parenthood

**Holding:** Upheld fetal-remains rule; did not reach "reason-based" ban. **Effect:** Validated ancillary regulatory terrain (chipping).

2020

June Medical v. Russo

**Holding:** Struck LA admitting-privileges law; fractured methodology. **Effect:** Preserved access temporarily, but Roberts' concurrence narrowed reasoning (chipping).

2020

Little Sisters v. Pennsylvania (Adjacent)

**Holding:** Upheld broad religious/moral exemptions to contraception mandate. **Effect:** More employer opt-outs, shifting costs to workers; extended *Hobby Lobby* (adjacent chipping).

2021

Whole Woman’s Health v. Jackson (SB 8)

**Holding:** Allowed only narrow pre-enforcement suit; law largely operated. **Effect:** Near-total cessation in-state pre-*Dobbs*; tolerated aggressive state design (chipping).

2022

Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health

**Holding:** Constitution confers no abortion right; **overruled *Roe* & *Casey***. **Effect:** Returned regulation to states; trigger bans took effect (OVERTURN).

2023-2025+

Post-Roe Legal Battlegrounds (Hostile Era)

**Issues:** Challenges to **FDA's authority** over medication abortion, and legal fights over state attempts to restrict **interstate travel** for care (e.g., NY/TX cases). **Effect:** Intense legal volatility and highly restricted access in many regions.
